Mr. 360

AB de Villiers

Some players follow the rules of cricket. AB de Villiers rewrote them. With bat in hand he became a magician, turning impossible angles into breathtaking strokes. A ball outside off stump might fly over fine leg. A yorker could vanish behind point. The field became his canvas, and every shot a brushstroke of genius. From Pretoria’s grounds to the world’s biggest stadiums, crowds leaned forward in disbelief. Because with AB, anything was possible. Fastest centuries, impossible chases, and innings that felt like fireworks exploding in daylight. Yet behind the brilliance lived humility — a player who smiled through pressure, who played not just to win, but to entertain the world. They called him Mr. 360°, for he struck the ball in every direction imaginable. But perhaps his greatest gift was reminding fans why cricket is magical. AB de Villiers — a cricketer who didn’t just play the game, but turned it into art.

Country: South Africa

Role: Middle-order batter

Why I admire him: He made impossible shots look normal and brought joy to the game.
