Hitman

Rohit Sharma

Where others fight the game, Rohit Sharma seems to float through it. His bat paints arcs in the air, and the ball travels like a bird set free. They call him the Hitman — yet there is poetry in his power. From the narrow lanes of Mumbai to world cricket’s grandest stages, Rohit grew not with noise, but with elegance. His pull shot crackles like thunder, his drives glide like wind across water. Three double centuries in One Day Internationals — a feat so extraordinary it sounds like legend, yet for Rohit it became reality. He does not rush greatness. He builds it patiently, like a sculptor chiseling marble. And when his innings blooms, the scoreboard becomes a garden of runs. As captain he carries calm like armor. Storms of pressure swirl around him, yet he stands composed, guiding his team forward. For Rohit Sharma is more than a run-machine — he is proof that cricket can still be beautiful. A master who shows that strength can wear the face of elegance.

Country: India

Role: Opening batter

Why I admire him: He makes batting look easy and can change a match in a few overs.
